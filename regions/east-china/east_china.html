East China

East China is a key area of China historically, politically, and economically. A thousand years ago, this region served as the main terminus of the Maritime Silk Road that traded extensively with Japan and Southeast Asia. Today, it is the economic powerhouse of Asia.

One third of China’s Population: East China is the most densely populated region of China with 483/km². Many of the people in the large cities are migrant workers. The growth of the large cities in terms of buildings and roads as such is due to their work.

History: The importance of this area politically as well as historically goes back for thousands of years. Hangzhou and Suzhou served as capital cities before the unification under the Qing Dynasty. Before Beijing became the capital of China, Nanjing served as the capital for several dynasties and up to recent times.
