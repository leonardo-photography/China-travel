USE OF COOKIES

A cookie is a small file sent to your computer when you visit the site. It is stored on your computer’s hard drive by your web browser. Cookies are designed to improve the user experience by storing user preferences and other information, which is used to recognise you when you return. Most browsers accept cookies automatically, but usually you can alter the settings of your browser to prevent automatic acceptance. Please note that if you reject, refuse or disable the use of cookies some of the site’s functionality may be affected or lost. We may also use cookies to enable access to particular areas of the site after you have registered with us and to provide you with more tailored information when you return to the site. In certain circumstances we may also match cookies to personal information that you disclose to us or that we already hold. These circumstances include where you have registered and provided the personal information referred to above. We will use this information as set out in the section “What we do with your information” above.

If you are using another person’s computer to login to our site (e.g. in an internet cafe or a colleagues computer at work) then it’s possible your email address will display in the login field to a subsequent user of our site. We therefore advise customers to clear cookies stored by the web browser when using another person’s machine in order to prevent this from happening.

This site may display hyperlinks to third party websites, which may also use cookies. We have no access to or control over these cookies and you should consult the third party’s privacy policy for further information on their use of cookies and how to restrict them.

If you wish, you can set your browser to notify you before you receive a cookie so you have the chance to accept it and you can also set your browser to refuse to receive or send all cookies. The website www.allaboutcookies.org (run by the Interactive Marketing Bureau) contains step-by-step guidance on how cookies can be switched off by users. Please be aware that if you do not accept cookies our website may not function correctly or at all.
