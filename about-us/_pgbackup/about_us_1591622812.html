Why this site?

Welcome to Leonardo Lazo Photography, your gateway to a wonderful China experience. Why this site? As my wife and I enter our next phase in life, I know that we’re leaving a legacy for our children. We’ve been blessed to have experienced China in ways most people (including the Chinese) have not. Our goal for this site is to offer the world a window through which they can discover a China many have not seen.

A Little about ourselves

My name is Leo and my wife is Melissa whom I met when I first came to China in 1984. We’ve been married for over 33 years and what a journey it has been. My wife and I are both teachers by profession (or taught until my retirement late 2019). I’ve been teaching in China off and on since 1984. Since 1995, I've been teaching at YCIS. I’ve witness the transformation of this once small fishing village of Shanghai (literally “on the sea”) into a financial hub of the world. It boasts of having the second and eleventh tallest buildings in the world. I remember when Pudong was nothing but farmland. I’ve always had a love of photography, but it wasn’t until the last 15 years or so that I began to hone my skills. China offered fertile field to hone in my skills. So my family and I have been traveling around China documenting its beautiful places and wonderful people.

Our Charity Endeavors

Huge Grace Orphanage

Having lived in China for over 30 years, we’ve seen the great need among the people. One of those needs was orphanage work. My wife has been actively supporting Huge Grace Orphanage for several decades. These children range in disability severity and conditions. While they have food to eat, they do not have access to great food like lean meat and good quality seafood. So for many years, once a month, Melissa has buys meat and seafood. She also makes sure that they have their daily necessities to cook with like oil and soy sauce. Melissa has also taken a team of volunteers to engage the children in craft work, story telling and singing. Please take a look at inside Huge Grace Orphanage and be ready to be impacted!

Seeds of Hope

Another endeavor I have been involved for over 10 years is Seeds of Hope. SoH sprouted from the devastating earthquake that rocked Sichuan in 2008 where many lives (many of them students) were lost. The YCIS community bonded together and raised funds to help rebuild a primary school in rural area that was destroyed by the quake. Since then, YCIS has helped rebuilt or remodeled 7 schools throughout China. I’d like to invite you to explore the impact SoH has had in the lives of the students as well as in their communities. You can read more about Seeds of Hope.

Our Community

People are relational beings. We know how important it is to develop relationships with them. So one of our guiding principles is this quote:

“No one cares how much you know, until they know how much you care!”

Theodore Roosevelt

To that end, we take every opportunity to develop these relationships as we journey through life. Come let's take a stroll together and discover Our Community and the people that serve us.

Our Services

Since I began photographing the places we’ve visited, I have amassed thousands of photos. Nearly five years ago, Melissa and I came up with the idea of creating bilingual calendars (English and Chinese) with the photos I’ve taken. Every year I have a different theme and each month contains an inspirational message. We use the proceeds to help Huge Grace Orphanage or any other charitable work in need. Please visit my Services to see what we have to offer.

It’s our desire that this site would inspire you to explore China’s beauty and get to know it’s people. We would love to hear from you. All our contact details can be found here.
