About us

Welcome to Leonardo Lazo Photography, your gateway to a wonderful China experience.

My name is Leo and my wife is Melissa whom I met when I first came to China in 1984. We’ve been married for over 33 years and what a journey it has been.

Who we are

My wife and I are both teachers by profession (or taught until my retirement late 2019). I’ve been teaching in China off and on since 1984. Since 1995, I've been teaching at YCIS. I’ve witness the transformation of this once small fishing village of Shanghai (literally “on the sea”) into a financial hub of the world. It boasts of having the second and eleventh tallest buildings in the world. I remember when Pudong was nothing but farmland. I’ve always had a love of photography, but it wasn’t until the last 15 years or so that I began to hone my skills. China offered fertile field to hone in my skills. So my family and I have been traveling around China documenting its beautiful places and wonderful people.

Our Work with Charity

Having lived in China for over 30 years, we’ve seen the great need among the people. One of those needs was orphanage work. My wife has been actively supporting Huge Grace Orphanage for several decades. These are children ranging in severity and conditions. While they have food to eat, they do not have access to great food like lean meat and shrimps. So for many years, once a month, Melissa has brought lean meat, shrimps. She also makes sure that they have their daily neccessities to cook like oil and soy sauce. Melissa has also taken a team of volunteers to engage the children in craft work, story telling and singing.

Another charitable work I have been involved for over 10 years is Seeds of Hope. SoH was the outgrowth of the devastating earthquake that rocked Sichuan in 2008 where many lives (many of htem students)were lost. YCIS community were moved to help so they raised funds to help rebuilt a primary school in rural area that was destroyed by the quake. Since then, YCIS has been raising funds to help primary schools in other areas. Up to now, YCIS has helped rebuilt 7 schools throughout China. But my work with these SoH schools has been to take groups of our students to these school to teach them English through various activities. We have also helped these school with attaining equipment like new desks and chairs and stationary for the students.

All in all, we know how important it is to develop relationship with people. So one of my guiding principles is this quote: “No one cares how much you know, until they know how much you care!”-Theodore Roosevelt. So our aim is to let others know how much we care through our service and relationships. It is our hope that life are impacted through what we do.

Calendars

Since I began photographing the places we’ve visited, I have amassed thousands of photos. Nearly five years ago, Melissa and I came up with the idea of creating bilingual calendars (English and Chinese) with the photos I’ve taken. Every year I have a different theme and each month there is an inspirational message. We use the proceeds to help Huge Grace Orphanage or any other charitable work in need. Please visit my Shop to see what we have to offer.

It’s our desire that this site would inspire you to explore China’s beauty and get to know it’s people. We would love to hear from you. All our contact details can be found here.
