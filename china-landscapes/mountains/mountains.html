The People's Republic of China

China's Vastness

To fully appreciate China's splendor, one must grasp its vastness.

Population: While the US is slightly bigger in terms of area (if you include Alaska), China has a whopping 1.1 billion more people than the US.

Rivers: It has two of the world's 5 longest rivers.

Ethnicity: It has more than 56 ethnic groups within its borders. The Han comprises the majority.

Language: “Three dialects split from the accepted seven, Seven official dialects within its borders, One dialect to rule them all, One dialect to find them, One dialect to bring them all and in this great vastness unite them. In the Land of the Han where the Great Wall lies.” Mandarin is not only the official language of China, but it is the most spoken language in the world with 1.5 billion speakers. While spoken language may not be understood, most in China understand the written language. There are two types of written characters: simplified (used in China and Singapore and going left to right) and traditional (used by HK, Taiwan and most of the Chinese overseas and going top to bottom and right to left). If that is not enough to boggle the mind, you would need to know at least 5,000 characters to be able to read the newspaper. There are more than 20K characters. And they all have to be memorized with the correct stroke order. Our 26 letters is a walk in the park.

Cuisine: While every region has its "specialties", Chinese cuisine falls within these eight: Anhui, Cantonese, Fujian, Hunan, Jiangsu, Shandong, Sichuan and Zhejiang. Sichuan is probably the most world renowned cuisine and is very spicy. For those that like spicy, there is a Chinese saying, "I'm not afraid of being spicy, I'm afraid of NOT being spicy!"

Landscape: Deserts, grasslands, mountains and tropical coastlines all combine to form China. China’s sweeping landscape harbours some of the world’s most extreme climates, as well as truly inspiring natural wonders. Zhangjiajie’s towering peaks inspired Avatar. It is home to part of the world’s fifth largest desert. Giant pandas can only be found in the mountains of Central China. 40% of the country is natural grassland. One third of the country’s land is made up of mountains. It shares a border with 14 nations. Home to the world’s highest peak of Mount Everest at 8,848 metres and the peaceful Ayding Lake, which sits 154 metres below sea level, China has the greatest altitude difference on Earth.
