The Drapers Arms

The Draper's Arms is one of Islington's best-loved and longest-established gastropubs. Walking that fine line between pub and restaurant, The Drapers Arms seems able to pull off the trick of offering something that pleases all customers. Something of a chameleon venue, it seems to be whatever you want it to be. Where this might seem a flaw to some, because it derives mainly from the high standard of service, it is instead endearing.

Two essential ingredients of what makes a pub a pub (as opposed to a restaurant) is the ability to keep and serve quality beers and a nice selection of friendly regulars. The Draper's Arms excels at both. There is also a lovely peaceful courtyard garden for long summer nights with bottles of wine (from an excellent wine list) and a brilliant line-up of regular beers and guest ales, all very well handled.

The menu changes with the seasons and while taking a steer from classic fine dining always manages to serve up an enjoyable surprise. Knowledgeable, friendly staff cap things off to make this a genuinely enjoyable restaurant or pub night out. Dogs are fussed throughout the pub and water bowls and occasional treats are provided.

Superior Islington gastropub, with excellent menu but enough tradition to still serve a decent pint

Food & drink

Seasonal, fresh, local (ish), sustainable ingredients cleverly combined for a knowing bunch of London foodies. Top notch.
