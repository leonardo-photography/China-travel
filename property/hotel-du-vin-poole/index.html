Hotel du Vin Poole

Poole's HdV is located in the former Mansion House in the old medieval port in a colourful, Georgian mansion enveloped in a wonderful Virginia creeper.

Eat: Dogs are allowed in all the public areas, with the exception of the restaurant (Bistro du Vin). Food in the bistro is exactly what you expect from a modern interpretation of French bistro food, all made to a consistently high standard with a wine list to match the hotel’s name. Better sommeliers than you’d expect at this standard of hotel are also a nice feature. The menu from the Bistro can be served on request in the lovely, cosy Crow's Next (complete with roaring fire in winter) or the bar. Room service is also available. 

Sleep: All bedrooms are dog-friendly and all carry the trademark HdV signature style, with the minimum standard being a bloody comfortable bed and a cracking shower. Dog owners will relish the "double terrace" rooms, which boast a private terrace or small garden.

Walk: Let's be brutal, most dog owners staying in Poole aren't interested in the town itself, but rather those incredible beaches nearby. And with 3 miles of golden sands to explore, who can blame them? Dogs are allowed on several of the beaches all year round, including Shell Beach at Studland and parts of Sandbanks (worth a walk to see some of the UK's most expensive homes). If you do want a more challenging walk, explore a section of the South West Coast Path (try the circular walk, Chapman's Pool) and the surrounding Purbeck countryside.

Off the Old Quay in Poole, this HdV is set in a Georgian townhouse covered with a fabulous Virginia creeper

Food & drink

Bistro du Vin offers a modern take on classic French-style cuisine, while there's also a popular bar, wine cellar and Laroche tasting room
