Cornwall

The Old Coastguard

The latest addition to the three-inn chain run by EatDrinkSleep, The Old Coastguard at Mousehole boasts the best views of the three. In fairness, it boasts better views than most pubs or hotels in the country. Owner Charles Inkin is responsible for the wonderful concept of the "Sleepover Sunday" (available here and at the other establishments in the group). Sleepover Sundays include a cracking Sunday lunch, a relaxing afternoon walking or with the papers (but without the “Sunday fear”), a supper, a good night’s sleep and breakfast on Monday morning.

Eat: Head chef Tom Symons relies on the fabulous local ingredients to serve up seasonal classics with flair. His maxim that “the best things in life are simple” says everything you need to know about the excellent food and drink on offer. Dogs are allowed in the bar and all outdoor areas. There is no extra charge for dogs, but it’s worth mentioning you’re bringing one when you book, as they will make sure you get a table for all your meals with space for the dog to lie down next to you as you eat.

Sleep: Dogs are welcome in all 14 bedrooms, most of which enjoy views out over the Cornish Coast, along to famous landmarks such as St Michael’s Mount and the Lizard. The ambition here is simple: “To provide a modern version of a traditional inn [that] feeds and waters the traveller before affording them the best night’s sleep they remember.”

Walk: It's not difficult to either work up an appetite or knacker yourself out along the coastal path here. The air is always fresh and walks are usually bracing. From October to Easter you can walk dogs down to Mousehole Beach, from where your can also pick up the South West Coast Path. Other beaches that are dog-friendly all year round include the sandy Mount’s Bay, Eastern Green and Longrock. Don't forget that Land's End, the most south-westerly point of mainland Britain, is also not far away.
