The Gurnard’s Head

One of three “dining pubs with rooms” run by EatDrinkSleep, The Gurnard’s Head is located on Cornwall’s impressive Atlantic coast between St Ives and St Just. The aim, as with all of Charles Inkin’s pubs, is to return guests to a slower, more relaxed way of life. To do this he sets out to recreate what he describes as “a modern version of a traditional inn [that] feeds and waters the traveller before affording them the best night’s sleep they remember”. Inkin is also responsible for the wonderful concept of the Sleepover Sunday (available here and at the other establishments in the group). Sleepover Sundays include a cracking Sunday lunch, a relaxing afternoon walking or with the papers (but without the “Sunday fear”), a supper, a good night’s sleep and breakfast on Monday morning.

Eat: Head chef Bruce Rennie relies on the fabulous local ingredients to serve up seasonal classics with flair. His aim, he says, is to "take the best of the Cornish larder and approach them with simplicity but skill". Although the local fish and seafood features, there is plenty for non meat or fish eaters. Dogs are allowed in the bar and all outdoor areas. There is no extra charge for dogs, but it’s worth mentioning you’re bringing one when you book, as they will make sure you get a table for all your meals with space for the dog to lie down next to you as you eat.

Sleep: Dogs are welcome in all seven bedrooms, all of which attempt to combine the best of modern comforts (luxurious Vi-Spring beds) with the more relaxed feeling of a more innocent age. The ambition here is simple: “To provide a modern version of a traditional inn [that] feeds and waters the traveller before affording them the best night’s sleep they remember.”

Walk: A walk along the Cornish coastal path is always refreshing and usually bracing. The stretch between St Ives and St Just is also steeped in tin mining history and the South West Coast Path takes in several local sites. Or try going inland and walking the Geevor Tin Mine Circular or the Bosigran Walk. There also plenty of dog-friendly beaches, including Gurnard's Head, which is also known as Treen Cove.
